Key points relevant to this lesson:

- The Bank's main policy tool is **Bank Rate** (the "base rate") — the interest rate it
  pays to commercial banks, which influences the rates those banks charge households and
  businesses.
- Changes in Bank Rate feed through the economy via several channels: borrowing and saving
  decisions, asset prices, expectations, and the exchange rate.
- This transmission is **not immediate**. The Bank notes that changes in interest rates
  take time to have their full effect — often estimated at around **one to two years** —
  because contracts, loans, wages and prices adjust gradually. Economists describe these
  as **"long and variable lags."**
- Because of the lag, policymakers set rates based on where they expect inflation to be in
  the future, not only where it is today.
