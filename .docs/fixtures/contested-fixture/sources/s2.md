Key points relevant to this lesson:

- The ECB's **primary objective** is to maintain **price stability** in the euro area. This is
  defined in the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and cannot be overridden by
  the ECB itself.
- The ECB's Governing Council has defined price stability as a 2% inflation target over the
  medium term (as measured by the Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices, HICP).
- Without prejudice to price stability, the ECB supports the general economic policies of the
  European Union, which include high employment. However, **this secondary support is only
  active once the primary mandate is satisfied** — price stability comes first by Treaty design.
- The ECB notes that maintaining price stability is itself the best contribution monetary policy
  can make to long-run employment and growth, because it avoids the uncertainty costs that
  high or volatile inflation imposes on investment and wage-setting.
- Unlike the Federal Reserve, the ECB does not have an explicit employment objective; employment
  is a downstream benefit of the primary mandate, not a co-equal goal.
