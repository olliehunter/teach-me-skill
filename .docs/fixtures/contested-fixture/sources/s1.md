Key points relevant to this lesson:

- The Federal Reserve Act requires the Fed to promote **maximum employment, stable prices, and
  moderate long-term interest rates**. The first two are commonly called the "dual mandate."
- Both goals are treated as **co-equal**. The Fed explicitly states it does not subordinate
  employment to price stability or vice versa.
- In practice, when the two goals conflict — for example, cutting rates to support employment
  would risk higher inflation — the Fed must weigh them against each other and make a judgment
  about which risk is larger at that moment.
- The Fed notes that maximum employment is a "broad-based and inclusive" goal, and that the
  level of maximum employment is not directly observable but is estimated from various
  labour-market indicators.
- Price stability is defined by the FOMC as a 2% inflation target over the longer run (as
  measured by the price index for personal consumption expenditures, PCE).
